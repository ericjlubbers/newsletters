DAILY DIGEST

John Leyba, The Denver Post

Your daily resource for late-breaking news, upcoming events, the Denver weather forecast, Denver traffic information, and the stories that will be talked about on Wednesday, Nov. 26, 2014. Download our iPhone, iPad and Android apps for breaking news throughout the day.

1. BREAKDOWN: KIDS ARE LEAST LIKELY TO RECEIVE EARLY TREATMENT FOR MENTAL ILLNESS

Joshua Plunkett's mind has been different since he was born. He is among the 13 percent of children who have diagnosable mental illness, and an example of how early treatment can curb the impact of mental problems on school and growing up. Read the final part of our four-part investigative series.

2. ARRESTS MADE, PEPPER-SPRAY USED DURING DENVER FERGUSON PROTESTS

When demonstrators gathered across the street from the Capitol about 6 p.m., organizers admitted they didn't exactly know what was going to happen. As it turned out, the night served to highlight a variety of grievances. Meanwhile, a review of Ferguson grand jury testimony doesn't clarify crucial details about the confrontation that left Michael Brown dead.

3. "IT'S REAL," DENVER RESIDENTS SAY ABOUT RACIAL BIAS AMONG POLICE

While race relations in Denver may not be as heated as they are in other cities, fear exists among minorities — even if they haven't done anything wrong — when they encounter police.

4. COLORADO THANKSGIVING TRAVELERS SHOULD EXPECT SNOW, CONGESTION

It's the busiest travel day of the year and those planning to traverse the state and country should be ready for winter weather and lots of congestion.

5. DENVER MARIJUANA SHOPS JOINING BLACK FRIDAY FRENZY WITH DEALS AND DISCOUNTS

The eager-to-normalize marijuana industry is ready to cash in on the weekend after Thanksgiving — just like everyone else. Read a report from Ricardo Baca, The Post's marijuana editor.

6. PUEBLO AUTHORITIES SHOOT, KILL MAN DURING ASSAULT INVESTIGATION

Law enforcement officers in Pueblo investigating an assault shot and killed an armed man on Tuesday night at a gas station.

7. WOMAN WHO BILKED PUBLIC BY CLAIMING SON HAD CANCER IS SENTENCED

An Aurora woman who received tens of thousands of dollars and a free trip to Disneyland after pretending her six-year-old son had cancer was sentenced Tuesday to 90 days in jail and five years probation.

8. COMMERCE CITY POLICE ID SUSPECTS SHOT BY DENVER COPS; DETAILS SPARSE

Authorities on Tuesday identified two brothers wounded by Denver police officers on Thursday night in Commerce City, saying both will face charges in the events preceding the shooting.

9. NEED A LYFT TO DIA? RIDE-SHARING SERVICE NOW CAN TAKE YOU TO THE AIRPORT

Just in time for Thanksgiving, passengers will be able hail a Lyft car to and from Denver International Airport after an agreement was reached between the ride-sharing service and the airport.

10. BRONCOS SIGN KICKER CONNOR BARTH, WAIVE BRANDON MCMANUS

Welcome, Connor Barth, to Broncos football, where every game seemingly has playoff implications, starting Sunday night against the Kansas City Chiefs at Arrowhead Stadium.
