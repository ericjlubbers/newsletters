ENTERTAIN ME!

Cobbled together by Eric Lubbers: elubbers@denverpost.com / @brofax

Listen, I know it's Monday. But the holiday season is in full swing, the weather is shaping up to be spectacular this week and the Broncos won yesterday, so it's not all bad, right?

In today's Roundup, we'll dive a little deeper into the Broncos gritty win, visit with a Denver city worker still going strong six decades into her career, and find out why some people are still clinging to their flip phones. Let's get started.

Broncos drain Chargers

33 min. 45 sec.

That's how long the Broncos and Chargers went without putting a single point on the board yesterday. I'm not sure the TV audiences would have stuck around after the Chargers' field goal with 3:45 left in the first half if they would have known it was the last scoring play of the game.

The low-flying, defense-heavy win means the Broncos can push for the top playoff seed in the AFC. Our beat writers ranked the best/worst of the game, Mark Kiszla said young Brock Osweiler is doing fine, as long as he has that defense backing him up and Woody Paige noticed the only thing that brought Broncos and Chargers fans together was seeing the Patriots lose on the Jumbotron. Head over to our Broncos section to grade the team for yourself or relive the magic with our photo gallery.

The Big Stuff

Pueblo looking to join in on Colorado's boom. Pueblo's history is in its heavy industry, mostly in the steel mill that is a shell of its glory days, but residents and city officials are looking to new, diverse businesses to turn the ninth-largest city in the state into a hub for hi-tech industry, artists and tourism. Alicia Wallace has the whole story.

"I say, 'I'm tired. I'm sleepy. I don't feel like being here. I'll be glad when it's Friday.' A lot of us do that. I've never heard Albertine say that. How does she do it? How does she get up to do this every day? That makes me get up."

Kim Stewart, a co-worker of 80-year-old Albertine Sellers

She keeps going and going and going... Noelle Phillips had a nice long talk with Albertine W. Sellers, an 80-year-old who has worked for the city of Denver for 60 years and is showing no signs of stopping. For those keeping score at home, that means she started in 1965 and has served under 10 different Denver mayors. Keep that in mind at around 2:30 when you start thinking that this afternoon will never end.

The tiny (but sturdy) flip phone crowd. Everybody knows one of them. Sure, you've been mashing your smartphone screen for the better part of a decade, but there's always that one friend/relative/co-worker that is holding on to their trusty Nokia. Or Samsung. Or LG. They're not alone, but their numbers are shrinking as fewer flip phones are manufactured and the cheap end of the smartphone pool keeps getting better.
