Equine Center Volunteers

Winter is here, which means the number of abused and neglected horses coming to the Harmony Equine Center, located in Franktown, will be increasing. The Dumb Friends League is seeking volunteers who can muck stalls, feed the horses and provide these wonderful animals with loving care until they find new homes. The Harmony Equine Center is a private rehabilitation and adoption facility for horses, ponies, donkeys and mules that have been removed from owners' care by law enforcement authorities. Adoption days are every Friday, Saturday and Sunday from 11 a.m. to 3 p.m. or by appointment. To learn more visit www.ddfl.org/equine. 


 

Walk For A Dog

Support your local animal organization simply by walking your dog. Walk for a Dog is a smartphone app that supports your local animal organization every time you walk your dog. After you download the app onto your smartphone, take the phone with you when you walk your dog(s). Simply press the "Start Walking for ..." button and the app will keep track of your walk. When your walk is stopped it is credited to your selected shelter or rescue. The more people walking for that organization, the more they donate.

For information and to download visit here.

Teaching Your Cat
To "Sit"

Training your cat to work for his or her food isn't a bad thing. In fact, it can act as more of a reward. Maintaining positive reinforcement and patience, getting your cat to sit will help build its character. And it can help with a cat that may have trouble with distractions.
