Happy Wednesday afternoon, folks. I hope you weren't scared by that glowing orb in the sky this morning. That's what we used to call "the sun" before the clouds took over the state. But now, we're on the on-ramp to temperatures in the 80s by the weekend.

We're busy getting ready for the relaunch of denverpost.com next week, so we have no time to waste. Let's get to it.

The Big Stuff

“I’ve never seen anything like it. Someone manufactured these things. It took some effort to dig those holes and put those in there and they were spaced out a good bit. Somebody was trying to do some real harm and they were targeting bikers.”

Tim Fishback, mountain biker

Listen all y'all, it's a sabotage. We're not talking about the Beastie Boys here, folks, we're talking about concrete bricks with 3-inch nails buried along the Buffalo Creek mountain bike trail. Jason Blevins has the whole story of suspected sabotage on one of the newest and most popular mountain biking trails in Colorado.

“This is Golden! This isn’t supposed to happen here!”

Jessica Swift, whose husband was stabbed in a botched robbery

Golden stabbing assailants went to the wrong house. The three men who barged into the Golden home of middle school teachers Jessica and Jesse Swift, armed with knives and looking for $10,000 in cash and drugs, were apparently at the wrong house, according to Golden Police. Jesse was stabbed multiple times and managed to fight back against one of his assailants — who is now in custody — with a kitchen knife. Police are on the lookout for the other two men. You can't miss Tom McGhee's full story on this bizarre case, including the full audio of Jessica's 911 call.

One of these guys will challenge Michael Bennet in November. Last night, downstairs in The Denver Post auditorium, we hosted a debate between the five men who will be battling it out in the June primary to be the Republican candidate for Colorado's Senate seat. I can't begin to summarize the whole thing (one candidate said the solution to health care is to get Coke and Pepsi executives in a room to hammer something out) but John Frank's excellent recap and Daniel Petty's "10 memorable moments from the debate" (or just watching the whole thing for yourself) will get you caught up.

By The Numbers

0

That's the number of days that U.S. 40 on Berthoud Pass — a historically avalanche-prone stretch of high country road — was closed to drivers this winter, thanks to the French-made Gazex exploders installed by CDOT to remotely trigger small avalanches to prevent the big ones from disrupting service. Jason Blevins has a story on the now-successful new system.
