It’s Trump

Donald Trump will be America's 45th president. The man who over the course of his campaign was wholly written off by the media (and pollsters), endorsed by white supremacists, praised by Putin and ran on a platform that included banning members of an entire religion from entering the US won the Electoral College handily last night. But how?

The answer, oversimplified, is working class white people who felt left behind by the economy and looked down upon by the establishment who saw him and felt a stronger connection than policy wonks or the media dared to imagine. So while Hillary Clinton looks to have the votes of more Americans, Trump will take the White House.

And with it, the GOP also retained control of the Senate and the House of Representatives, plus at least one Supreme Court seat.

This morning, Hillary Clinton conceded the race to Donald Trump, saying "We owe him an open mind and a chance to lead."

Meanwhile, in Colorado

+ Colorado managed to be one of the only "swing" states that actually still voted for Hillary.

+ Michael Bennet will again be a Colorado Senator.

+ Every Colorado incumbent to the House of Representatives won, including Mike Coffman and Scott Tipton.

+ The GOP is hoping to hang on to their one-vote edge in the state Senate as the Democrats keep their lead in the House.

+ Denver voted to give more funding to schools, while Jefferson County denied a similar measure.

+ The Denver Metro area said a resounding yes to the SCFD arts and cultural district.

+ Denver has its first female district attorney in Beth McCann and Jeffco's Pete Weir looks to be surviving a well-funded challenge.

+ Soda will be getting more expensive in Boulder, one of four cities to pass sugary drink taxes nationwide.

By The Numbers

2%

Two percent of Colorado voters didn't vote for president at all, one of the biggest so-called "undervotes" since at least 2004.

Colorado's changing constitution

+ Amendment 69: There will be no ColoradoCare.

+ Amendment 70: Colorado's lowest wage workers will (eventually) see a $12 minimum wage.

+ Amendment 71: Colorado's constitution is about to get a lot harder to amend.

+ Amendment 72: Tobacco won't get an extra tax.

+ Amendment T: Voters are leaning toward leaving a "slavery" reference in the state's constitution.

Welcome to the party, unaffiliated voters

+ Goodbye caucuses, hello primaries as Props 107 and 108 are headed to victory.
