BLOTTER: DON'T TRY TO KIDNAP AN MMA FIGHTER

ARVADA — A man with an admitted history of gang activity told police he was walking to work on Nov. 26 in the 5300 block of Carr Street, Arvada, when a woman in a black Mazda asked if he wanted a ride. After getting into the back seat he was promptly put into a chokehold by another passenger as the woman pulled out a knife. He elbowed the woman, broke her nose, then backfisted her jaw. He then took the arm of the male and snapped it over his shoulder. He escaped and reported the attack, telling police he was a mixed martial arts fighter. BUSINESS Q&A: Evolve Action Sports Park, Denver

Seth McConnell, YourHub

"Colorado offers many outdoor riding opportunities for free. Early in our planning process we realized we need to offer things that free parks can't. We narrowed this down to two key elements. The first is controling the positive attitude and vibe of what goes on inside our park. You can't make that happen at a public place. The second item is a foam pit and resi jump line, or soft safety ramp. These two elements allow riders to safely learn new tricks and evolve their riding. Evolve will have the only public foam pit and resi line on the Front Range." — Chris Oliver, General Manager

BUSINESS Q&A: The Den Restaurant, Evergreen

Seth McConnell, YourHub

"We have something for everyone. Breakfast served all day and real potatoes used for our country potatoes and Den-Fries. We have pizzas that have fresh ingredients and piled high. Burgers, "Den-Wiches" (sandwiches), pasta and homemade pies. We are in a cozy spot in the middle of Conifer and Evergreen." — Lisa Colonell-Kastner, co-owner
