BIRD REHAB RAISING FUNDS FOR NEW WHEAT RIDGE FACILITY

Denver Post file

After being forced to vacate its former space in southwest Denver two years ago, Wild Bird Information and Rehabilitation is hoping to raise $150,000 to open their new facility in Wheat Ridge in time for springtime bird migrations.

JEFFCO STUDYING MARIJUANA TESTING FACILITIES

It's unlikely anyone would accuse Jefferson County commissioners of rushing when it comes to deciding a marijuana policy, but one issue could be settled soon: a proposal to allow marijuana testing facilities in the unincorporated parts of the county.

OLD CHRISTMAS TREES ARE A GIFT FOR BEAR CREEK'S FISH

Courtesy Lakewood Parks and Golf Department

Christmas will keep on giving to fish and anglers alike at Bear Creek Reservoir thanks to a new program that puts last year's Christmas trees to use improving fish habitat.

AURORA NONPROFIT TO RENOVATE LOW-INCOME APARTMENTS

Officials in Aurora are working to increase the number of updated affordable and transitional housing options, and working with nonprofits like Community Housing Partners to renovate buildings to provide homes is one way to combat the "extraordinarily low" vacancy rate.

DOUGCO LIBRARIES' ADVENTURE PASS GRANTS ACCESS TO CULTURE

A library card can get much more than a book or a DVD in Douglas County, as its "Adventure Pass" gives cardholders free admission to places like the Butterfly Pavilion or the History Colorado Center.

BUSINESS Q&A: SUNNYSIDE BURGER BAR, DENVER

Seth McConnell, YourHub

"All of our burgers are chef-inspired (Troy Guard designed our entire menu) and we get only the freshest ingredients available. We offer some pretty interesting combos, such as pimiento cheese, spinach and Tenderbelly bacon on our Hipster Burger, or roasted beet root, basil aioli, arugula and goat cheese on our Hippy Dippy Burger. We hand-cut our french fries out of Kennebec potatoes. Even our sriracha is a local, custom-made product." - Kim Zeylmaker, general manager
