Jeffco commissioners reluctant to consider bear-proof trash ordinance

Some residents in Evergreen want the county to adopt a trash ordinance following a recent incident in which a resident shot and killed two bear cubs when they were getting into his trash. "To me the incident that happened isn't an isolated incident, it's something that's been brewing for years," said Emma Channing, an Evergreen resident involved in the effort to introduce an ordinance.

Cherry Hills Village Barn Tour opens doors to support open space

The Cherry Hills Land Preserve is inviting people to support preservation of the outdoors by heading indoors at a collection of Cherry Hills Village barns this weekend. The fifth annual Cherry Hills Village Barn Party, Farmers Market & Tour will take place this weekend with all proceeds benefitting the Land Preserve and its mission of protecting and enhancing open lands in the suburban Arapahoe County community.

Anya Semenoff, YourHub

Free fitness festival in Aurora aims to increase rec center membership

More residents are relying on city-owned fitness and recreation centers for their workouts thanks to restructured prices that outdo private gyms and free days that highlight a growing diversity of classes and wellness activities. The city will host a free fitness festival at the Beck Recreation Center and the Aurora Center for Active Adults on Sept. 18 as part of an ongoing effort to increase membership at recreation centers citywide.

Blotter: Drunken cyclist blocks street sweeper

A street sweeping crew reported a bicyclist was causing a disturbance Sept. 3 while they were working in the area of West Colfax Avenue and Pierce Street in Lakewood. The cyclist stopped in front of the crew and refused to move after being asked to do so several times. Police learned the man had been drinking at a nearby park. After being arrested for violating a protection order, the man became hostile, spitting and coughing on the officers. Read more police reports at http://denverpost.com/blotter.
