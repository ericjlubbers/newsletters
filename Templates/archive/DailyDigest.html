THE DAILY DIGEST

Good afternoon! Here are the top stories from The Denver Post for Monday, Sept. 28, 2015, including some you may have missed over the weekend.

1. How Colorado's unemployment rate is hurting holiday hiring

Colorado's low unemployment rate — 4.2 percent — is good news, of course, but retailers and shipping companies looking to staff up for the holidays are finding it hard to get workers. Recruiters are going so far as to get out to churches, community groups and colleges while offering tuition assistance, bus passes and more perks to get qualified candidates.

2. The Broncos are 3-0

It's a familiar refrain: The Broncos' offense looked weak compared to last year ( you can grade them here in our report card), but appeared improved in Week 3 and delivered when it counted against Detroit. Once again, though, the defense starred, causing destruction with its takeaways in the 24-12 victory. The win came thanks to big plays from reserves: safety David Bruton, outside linebacker Shaquil Barrett and receiver Bennie Fowler. Quarterback Peyton Manning found himself more often in a pistol formation, which seemed to work, columnist Woody Paige writes from Detroit. Unfortunately, Von Miller and Demaryius Thomas are likely next in line for NFL fines.

3. Speaking of football, the fantasy game is getting big money — and scrutiny

Lone Tree's Peter Jennings is just like that guy you're seeing on all the Draft Kings and Fan Duel TV commercials: He actually won $1 million last year playing fantasy football. "It's just like investing, but instead of stocks you're trading in athletes," said Jennings, a former stock trader who was the game's first six-figure winner on a $10 entry fee in 2012. But is it a game of skill, or gambling?

4. Being transgender in a conservative family

They thought this only happened in ultra-liberal families, that transgender children were influenced by wayward parents. But a teenager's announcement that she is transgender sent her family on a journey of faith that resulted in a simple answer: unconditional love. Jennifer Brown and Mahala Gaylord bring the story of Alison.

5. There's liquid water on Mars

In 2008, scientists confirmed the existence of frozen water on Mars. But the latest data show that salt water — in liquid form — flows down certain Martian slopes each summer, according to NASA researchers. Images from the Mars Reconassiance Orbiter's camera — built at Boulder-based Ball Aerospace and Technologies Corp. — have repeatedly documented this.

6. More freestanding ERs are being built in Colorado — but mostly in wealthy neighorhoods

Colorado's hospital chains are increasingly building stand-alone emergency rooms near suburban shopping malls (20 have opened in Colorado since 2014). Hospitals argue it brings them closer to patients, where they live and work, which can save lives. But in the process, they're steering clear of low-income neighborhoods. And in reality, do they actually save lives, or just drive up insurance costs?

7. Will the funding come through?

A bill introduced by U.S. Rep. Jeff Miller, R-Fla., could jeopardize funding for a project that — as you might know — is way overbudget. It would allow the Department of Veterans Affiars to spend another $625 million — but only if $200 million comes out of awards and bonuses paid to VA employees.

8. Channel 9 may be out for DISH subscribers soon

The expiration of the current contract between KUSA's parent company TEGNA and DISH could mean a program blackout as early as Wednesday.

9. Denver toddler died by beating and burns

The Denver coroner ruled that the high-profile death of 23-month-old Jayvion Johnson, which resulted in changes at the city's human services department, resulted from blunt force injuries and burns, an autopsy released Monday showed.

10. Mechanical problems before plane crash

Authorities in southwest Colorado say the pilot of a small plane that crashed Friday near Pagosa Springs, killing both people aboard, reported mechanical problems before slamming into the ground.
