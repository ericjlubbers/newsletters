The life, struggle and eventual tragic death of 27-year-old Jack McAtee reads like something out of a Jon Krakauer novel. Indeed, Steve McAtee, Jack's father, said that during their year-long search, he and his family had sought clues from the author's work "Into the Wild." Kevin Simpson has a long look at the man and the impact of both his life and death on a family that never stopped searching.

The Big Stuff

State Rep. to try to curb hiring of second-chance cops. State Rep. Angela Williams is planning to push legislation to reduce the number of police officers getting work at Colorado agencies after being dismissed from previous jobs, including those with a history of violence.

+ This is the latest in a series by Christopher Osher about the issue of so-called "second-chance cops." Previously he's written about how Colorado laws allow rogue officers to bounce from department to department and the loopholes that give fired officers from other states a chance work in Colorado precincts.

Finally, a hint of autumn. Starting today, the weather in Colorado will finally feel like October, with a cool off that will last through the weekend, with some areas on the Eastern Plains dipping into freezing temperatures overnight Friday.

Is there such a thing as a "protest beer?" River North Brewery, named after the industrial neighborhood that became suddenly red-hot in Denver's booming real estate market, has been kicked out of its namesake neighborhood to make room for another apartment complex. To celebrate(?), the brewery is releasing a trio of beers called "Funk the Man" -- infused with hibiscus and black currant -- as their final hurrah.

By The Numbers

70,000

That's how many square feet of shopping that will be available in the new four-story RH (formerly Restoration Hardware) concept store that opens Friday as the anchor the largest expansion to the Cherry Creek Shopping Center in years.
